My journey

Born and raised in a small village in Bangladesh, I learned early that opportunity is precious. I graduated from a public school with a perfect 5.0 GPA in my final national exams, earning a highly competitive Russian Government Scholarship that brought me to study abroad. Now I work part‑time in Russia to support myself while continuing to give back.

My path has been shaped by a deep commitment to service: from volunteering with the Red Crescent to mentoring young students in programming and even tutoring two children who rose from the bottom of their class to the top. I believe that technology—especially open source—can be a powerful equalizer. I’ve been using Linux for five years, helping others in my community install and learn it for free. I aspire to contribute to the global Linux community and use my skills to solve real-world problems.
